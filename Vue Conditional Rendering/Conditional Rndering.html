Conditional Rendering (Hide and Show conditions)

Visible content

In Vue.js, directives are special attributes that allow you to apply special behavior to DOM elements. Directives are denoted by the `v-` prefix in the HTML template. Here are some common directives in Vue.js:

1. `v-if` and `v-show`: These directives are used for conditional rendering of elements. `v-if` will completely remove or add the element based on the condition, while `v-show` will toggle the element's visibility.

2. `v-for`: This directive is used to render a list of items based on an array or an object.

3. `v-bind` or `:`: This directive is used to dynamically bind an attribute to a value or an expression.

4. `v-on` or`@`: This directive is used to attach event listeners to elements and bind them to a method in the component.

5. `v-model`: This directive is used for two-way data binding between form input elements and the component's data.

6. `v-cloak`: This directive is used to hide the uncompiled Vue.js templates until they are compiled and ready to render. These are just a few of the directives available in Vue.js. You can also create your own custom directives to extend the functionality of Vue.js.
